Involute Spur Gear Suite

Geometry • Strength • STE (NN) • EHL Efficiency • Generation Lab • Theory • Exports

GEARCALC PRO is an educational/research platform for involute spur gears. It includes operating geometry, key constraint checks, AGMA and ISO 6336 strength calculations, a STE (NN) model with validated prediction accuracy of 97.5%, an EHL Efficiency page for LOA-resolved tribology and local/system power-loss estimation, a high-fidelity generation lab, full theory documentation, and CSV exports for FEA workflows.

What's inside

  • Geometry: base/addendum/root circles, profile shifts, tip thickness, undercut warnings.
  • Strength: bending & contact (AGMA) and root/contact checks (ISO 6336) with editable factors.
  • STE (NN): neural predictor for transmission error response curves.
  • EHL Efficiency: load distribution, sliding ratio, EHL friction and local/system power-loss trend along LOA.
  • Generation Lab: visualization of rack-generation and involute trace in the local frame.
  • Theory: full documentation of the formulas implemented in the tool (MathJax).
  • Exports: profile/tooth/full outline + FEA boundary segments.

Validation Philosophy

The platform combines analytical geometry, standardized strength calculations, visual generation mechanics, and neural surrogate prediction in a single environment. This makes it suitable for classroom use, lab demonstration, and early-stage engineering evaluation.

Computation Theory (Involute Spur Gears)

This section documents the equations currently implemented in GEARCALC PRO, with emphasis on geometric constraints, LOA-based load sharing, STE (NN) surrogate prediction, and EHL-resolved efficiency/power-loss modeling.

Scope

AGMA + ISO stress workflow

Contents

1) Operating Geometry

The core operating quantities are computed from module \(m\), teeth \((z_1,z_2)\), pressure angle \(\alpha\), and profile shifts \((x_1,x_2)\).

\[d_i = m z_i,\qquad r_i = \frac{d_i}{2},\qquad r_{b,i}=r_i\cos\alpha\]
\[r_{a,i}=r_i+(C_k+x_i)m,\qquad r_{f,i}=r_i-(C_f-x_i)m\]
\[\operatorname{inv}(\alpha_w)=\operatorname{inv}(\alpha)+\frac{2(x_1+x_2)}{z_1+z_2}\tan\alpha,\qquad a_w=\frac{m(z_1+z_2)}{2}\frac{\cos\alpha}{\cos\alpha_w}\]
\[L=\sqrt{r_{a1}^2-r_{b1}^2}+\sqrt{r_{a2}^2-r_{b2}^2}-a_w\sin\alpha_w,\qquad \varepsilon_\alpha=\frac{L}{\pi m\cos\alpha}\]

2) Load sharing and normal load distribution (LOA)

For \(1<\varepsilon_\alpha<2\), contact follows the classical double-single-double sequence along LOA.

\[\xi=\frac{s}{p_{b1}},\qquad 0\le \xi \le \varepsilon_\alpha\]
\[L=\varepsilon_\alpha p_{b1},\qquad \xi=\frac{z_1\psi}{\pi d_{b1}}\]
\[R_M(\xi)=\begin{cases}0.36+\dfrac{0.28}{\varepsilon_\alpha-1}\,\xi,&0\le\xi\le\varepsilon_\alpha-1\\[4pt]1,&\varepsilon_\alpha-1\le\xi\le1\\[4pt]0.36+\dfrac{0.28}{\varepsilon_\alpha-1}(\varepsilon_\alpha-\xi),&1\le\xi\le\varepsilon_\alpha\end{cases}\]

In double-contact zones \(R_M<1\), while in the single-contact interval \(R_M=1\).

3) Geometric checks in GEARCALC (Eq. 20-25)

\[g_{4,5}(\mathbf{x})=Z_i-\frac{2(1-x_i)}{\sin^2 a_0}\le 0\qquad\text{(Eq. 20)}\]
\[g_6(\mathbf{x})=\begin{cases}x_i-0.60\le0,&6\le Z_i\le10\\x_i-0.50+0.01Z_i\le0,&10<Z_i\le50\\x_i-1.00\le0,&50<Z_i\end{cases}\qquad\text{(Eq. 21)}\]
\[g_7(\mathbf{x})=\begin{cases}x_i-0.05(18-Z_i)\ge0,&6\le Z_i\le12\\x_i-0.0375(20-Z_i)\ge0,&12<Z_i\le20\\x_i-\dfrac{20-Z_i}{60}\ge0,&20<Z_i\le50\\x_i+0.50\ge0,&50<Z_i\end{cases}\qquad\text{(Eq. 22)}\]
\[g_{8,9}(\mathbf{x})=C_{f,i}-\frac{(1-C_{s,i})\pi}{2\tan a_0}\le0\qquad\text{(Eq. 23)}\]
\[g_{10,11}(\mathbf{x})=C_{c,i}-\frac{(1-C_{s,i})\dfrac{\pi}{2}-C_{f,i}\tan a_0}{\cos a_0-(1-\sin a_0)\tan a_0}\le0\qquad\text{(Eq. 24)}\]
\[g_{12,13}(\mathbf{x})=r_{k,i}-\sqrt{(r_{b,1}+r_{b,2})^2\sin^2 a_w+r_{b,i}^2\cos^2 a_w}\le0\qquad\text{(Eq. 25)}\]

4) Strength block (AGMA + ISO 6336)

\[\sigma_F^{AGMA}=\frac{F_tK_oK_vK_sK_HK_B}{b\,m\,J},\qquad \sigma_H^{AGMA}=Z_E\sqrt{\frac{F_tK_oK_vK_sK_HZ_R}{b\,d_1\,I}}\]
\[\sigma_{H0}^{ISO}=Z_EZ_HZ_\varepsilon Z_\beta\sqrt{\frac{F_t(u+1)}{b\,d_1\,u}},\qquad \sigma_{F}^{ISO}=\frac{F_t}{b\,m}Y_FY_S(K_AK_vK_{F\beta}K_{F\alpha})\]

6) EHL efficiency model (detailed overview)

The model resolves the entire LOA with local load share, sliding/rolling kinematics, lubrication state and local loss.

\[\xi=\frac{s}{L},\quad q(s)\leftarrow R_M(\xi),\quad F_n(s)=F_t\frac{q(s)}{\cos\alpha_w}\]
\[v_{sl}(s)=v_1(s)-v_2(s),\quad v_{roll}(s)=\frac{v_1(s)+v_2(s)}{2}\]
\[\mu_{EHL}(s)=\mu_{lim}\big(1-e^{-\alpha_{PV}p(s)|v_{sl}(s)|}\big)\,\Phi_\lambda(\lambda(s),R_q,\text{additives}),\qquad \lambda(s)=\frac{h_{min}(s)}{\sigma}\]
\[dP_{fric}(s)=\mu_{EHL}(s)F_n(s)|v_{sl}(s)|,\qquad P_{loss}=\int_A^E dP_{fric}(s)\,ds + P_{aux},\qquad \eta_{mesh}=1-\frac{P_{loss}}{P_{in}}\]

Lubricant family, ISO VG and oil temperature influence \(\eta_0\), then film thickness \(h_{min}\), then \(\mu_{EHL}\) and efficiency.
